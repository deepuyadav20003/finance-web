    Financial advisors in Pennsylvania may charge for their services in several ways, including:

    Fee-only: Charging a flat fee, or a percentage of assets under management for their services.

    Fee-based: Charging a combination of fees and commissions.

    Commission-based: Earning a commission on the financial products they sell to clients.

    Financial advisors and financial coaches in Pennslyvania differ in the services that they offer. Financial advisors offer financial services, including investment management, retirement planning, wealth management, etc., while financial coaches prioritize a client's mindset and behavior related to money. Instead of providing financial advice or services, coaches help clients develop healthier financial habits and try to transform the client's relationship with money for overall well-being.

    Before hiring a financial advisor in Pennsylvania, it's important to ask the right questions to ensure that they are a good fit for your needs and goals. Some questions to consider asking include:

    1. What are your qualifications and credentials?

    2. How do you charge for your services?

    3. What types of clients do you typically work with?

    4. Can you provide references or testimonials from current or past clients?

    5. How will you communicate with me and how often?

    6. What is your approach to risk management?
