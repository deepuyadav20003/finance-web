    Frequently Asked Questions

    The role of a financial advisor is to provide professional financial guidance, advice, and money management services to individuals or businesses. They assist clients in various financial matters, including investment management, retirement planning, wealth management, estate management, and more, depending on the needs of an individual. Financial advisors assess their client's financial goals and risk tolerance and stay updated on market trends, investment options, and regulatory changes to provide informed advice. Additionally, financial advisors often act as fiduciaries, putting their client's best interests first while providing ongoing support and monitoring to help clients achieve their financial objectives. To make sure your best interests are being considered, read more about the role of a fiduciary financial advisor and the support they can provide.

    Many financial advisors in Kentucky are also tax professionals or work closely with tax professionals to help clients with tax planning and optimization. Additionally, they can help you navigate market fluctuations and make informed decisions about buying and selling investments. If you are looking to improve your investment portfolio, read on about why professional investment management may be right for you.

    When choosing a financial advisor in Kentucky, it's crucial to avoid common mistakes such as overlooking credentials, neglecting to assess experience and track record, overlooking conflicts of interest, not seeking references or reviews, and neglecting to discuss the advisor's fee structure. By avoiding these mistakes, you can make a more informed decision and select a financial advisor who is qualified, experienced, unbiased, and aligned with your financial goals. To learn more, refer to our guide on choosing the best financial advisor in Kentucky.

    The cost of hiring a financial advisor in Kentucky varies depending on the advisor's experience, services, and fee structure. Meanwhile, other advisors charge an hourly rate, flat fees, or based on commission. It's important to understand the advisor's fee structure and any potential conflicts of interest before engaging in their services.     To check if a financial advisor in Kentucky has any disciplinary actions or complaints filed against them, you can use the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority's (FINRA) BrokerCheck tool.
