To effectively evaluate your financial advisor, always ask questions like:

– If I lose 10% of my investment in year one and make 10% back in year two, how much have I gained or lost?
– Can I pay you hourly, and how does that work?
– If interest rates go up, does the value of a bond fund increase or decrease?
– Do you have your own money in what you recommend to me? Can you prove that?

]]> Comment on Can You Open a Roth IRA After You Turn 60? by Jeffrey https://www.wiseradvisor.com/blog/retirement/roth-ira-age-limit/#comment-853 Fri, 24 Feb 2023 08:28:45 +0000 https://www.wiseradvisor.com/blog/?p=2691#comment-853 As someone who has benefited dramatically from starting a Roth IRA early, I highly recommend it to others. Investing in dividend-paying stocks within the Roth is wise since any dividends and capital gains earned are tax-free. This means it’s better to pay the taxes now rather than later. Starting early and allowing your investment to grow over time is essential, especially since you’re likely in a lower tax bracket now than in the future.

One of the benefits of a Roth IRA is that you can withdraw contributions tax-free before you reach age 59.5 without facing any penalties. This is not true for traditional IRAs or 401(k)s. Additionally, Roth IRAs are not subject to required minimum withdrawals during retirement.

Another advantage of investing in a Roth IRA is trading stocks and funds within the account without reporting them at tax time. I regret not starting a Roth IRA sooner, as it would have had more time to grow.

It’s important to note that if your employer offers a 401(k) match, you should take advantage of it before shifting funds to the Roth.
