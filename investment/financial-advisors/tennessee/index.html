    To find a list of registered financial advisors in Tennessee, you can utilize our Tennessee advisor directory as the majority of advisors listed there provide a range of services, such as retirement planning, investment management, estate planning, tax planning, insurance planning, and wealth management.     To check if a financial advisor in Tennessee has any disciplinary actions or complaints filed against them, you can use the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority's (FINRA) BrokerCheck tool. This tool enables individuals to complete a thorough background check on financial professionals like brokers, brokerage firms, financial advisors, investment advisors, advising firms, etc. The detailed information provided through BrokerCheck includes their employment history, certifications, licenses, and active or past violations within the last ten years so that you can make an informed decision while hiring an advisor. We recommend using this guide to know how to use BrokerCheck to evaluate a financial advisor’s credentials.

    During the initial consultation with a potential financial advisor in Tennessee, it's recommended to ask them about their experience, areas of expertise, investment philosophy, fee and compensation methods, and their approach to financial planning. You should also ask about their fiduciary duty, which means they are legally and ethically bound to act in your best interests. It's important to choose an advisor who communicates clearly and transparently and whose values align with your own.
